The CDL Combination Vehicles Knowledge Exam is an essential step for drivers seeking to operate combination vehicles. This specialized exam tests your knowledge of operating these vehicles, which typically consist of a combination of a tractor and one or more trailers.

## Overview of the Combination Vehicles Exam

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Purpose of the Exam">
    The exam assesses your understanding of combination vehicles, including coupling and uncoupling, vehicle inspection, and the unique driving characteristics of these vehicles.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Key Topics Covered">
    Topics include air brakes (if applicable), combination vehicle control, rollover risks, proper turning, and managing the vehicle's space, speed, and lane position.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

| Exam Feature           | Details                              |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Number of Questions    | Typically 20-30 multiple-choice      |
| Passing Score          | 80% or higher                        |
| Time Limit             | Varies by state, often around 60 minutes |
| Key Topics             | Combination vehicle control, air brakes (if applicable), rollover risks, coupling and uncoupling, vehicle inspection |
| Test Format            | Computer-based in most locations     |
| Retake Policy          | Varies by state; often a waiting period and additional fee for retakes |
| Prerequisite           | Valid CDL; specific requirements may vary by state |



## Preparing for the Exam

<Steps>
  <Step title="Study the Relevant Sections in the CDL Manual">
    Focus on the sections of your state's CDL manual that pertain specifically to combination vehicles.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Practice with Sample Questions">
    Familiarize yourself with the types of questions you might encounter on the exam through online resources like [Driving-Tests.org](https://driving-tests.org).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand Coupling and Uncoupling Procedures">
    Pay special attention to the process of safely coupling and uncoupling trailers, as this is a crucial aspect of operating combination vehicles.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sample Exam Questions

Test your knowledge with these sample questions.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Question 1: Rollover Risks">
    What factor most significantly increases the risk of rollover in a combination vehicle?
    - A) Type of cargo
    - B) Speed on turns and curves
    - C) Color of the vehicle
    - Correct Answer: B) Speed on turns and curves
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Question 2: Coupling">
    When coupling, the proper position of the fifth wheel is:
    - A) Tilted down towards the rear of the tractor
    - B) Tilted up towards the front of the tractor
    - C) Level with the ground
    - Correct Answer: C) Level with the ground
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
