<Tip>**In a hurry? Quick summary:** </Tip>

If you have a Commercial Driver's License (CDL), it's important to understand the implications of the Air Brakes Restriction. This article will explain what the Air Brakes Restriction means and how it can impact your driving qualifications.

## What is the Air Brakes Restriction?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Air Brakes Restriction Explained">
    The Air Brakes Restriction is applied to your CDL if you take your CDL skills test in a vehicle not equipped with air brakes. This restriction prevents you from operating commercial vehicles with air brake systems.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Implications of the Air Brakes Restriction

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Limitation on Vehicle Types" icon="ban">
    With this restriction, you're limited to driving commercial vehicles that do not have air brakes, which can restrict job opportunities.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Impact on Employment" icon="briefcase">
    Many commercial vehicles, especially larger trucks and buses, are equipped with air brakes, so having this restriction can limit your employability in these roles.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Removing the Air Brakes Restriction

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pass an Air Brakes Knowledge Test">
    Study for and pass a written knowledge test on air brakes at your local DMV.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete a Skills Test in an Air Brake-Equipped Vehicle">
    Successfully complete a CDL skills test in a vehicle that is equipped with air brakes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update Your CDL">
    Once you've passed the tests, you can have the restriction removed from your CDL.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## FAQs on Air Brakes Restriction

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is it difficult to remove the Air Brakes Restriction?">
    Removing the restriction requires some study and practice but is a straightforward process for those familiar with commercial vehicles.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I drive any air brake-equipped vehicle after removing the restriction?">
    Yes, once the restriction is removed, you can legally operate commercial vehicles with air brake systems.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

> **Pro Tip**: Gaining proficiency with air brakes not only expands your driving capabilities but also makes you more versatile and appealing to potential employers.

Having an understanding of the Air Brakes Restriction and knowing how to remove it can significantly widen your career opportunities as a commercial driver. For resources and practice tests on air brakes, visit [Driving-Tests.org](https://driving-tests.org).
