<Tip>**In a hurry? Quick summary:** </Tip>

The Doubles/Triples (T) Endorsement is a critical addition for CDL holders who intend to operate vehicles towing two or three trailers. This endorsement demonstrates your proficiency in handling larger, more complex vehicle combinations.

<Snippet file="doubles-triples-endorsement-diagram.mdx" />

## What is the Doubles/Triples Endorsement?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Overview of the T Endorsement">
    The T Endorsement authorizes CDL holders to operate commercial vehicles pulling double or triple trailers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Significance of the T Endorsement">
    It's vital for jobs requiring the transport of multiple trailers, ensuring drivers have the skills to manage the added length and weight safely.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Obtaining the Doubles/Triples Endorsement

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pass the Knowledge Test">
    Study and pass a [written test](/essentials/written-tests/doubles/triples) focused on the specifics of operating double and triple trailers, such as coupling and uncoupling, and handling.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete Skills Test">
    Demonstrate your ability to safely operate a double or triple trailer setup under various road conditions.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Meet State-Specific Requirements">
    Some states might require additional qualifications or endorsements.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Topics for the T Endorsement Exam

<CardGroup cols={2}>

## FAQs on the Doubles/Triples Endorsement

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How often do I need to renew my T Endorsement?">
    Like the P Endorsement, it usually needs renewal every few years, depending on state regulations.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are there weight restrictions with the T Endorsement?">
    Yes, there are specific weight restrictions based on the state and the type of trailers being towed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

> **Expert Tip**: Practice with experienced drivers or in training courses to build confidence in handling doubles and triples.
